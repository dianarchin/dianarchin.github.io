## 🔮 The Origin Story

I read cards before I read data.

In a past life I was a professional tarot reader — which, it turns out, is excellent training for analytics. You lay out what you're given, you look for the pattern in the mess, and you tell the person what it actually *means*. Same instinct, different deck.

These days I work in **product operations**, sitting where business and data meet — turning Jira backlogs, sprint work, and messy ticket data into the insights and process improvements that help teams make better decisions. I'm building toward **Business Analyst and Data Analyst** roles, where asking the right question matters as much as writing the right query.

## ✨ The Spread — Projects

A reading is only as good as what you draw. A few from my deck:

**🔮 [Reading a Messy Ticket Queue](https://dianarchin.hashnode.dev/reading-a-messy-ticket-queue-a-methodology-walkthrough)** — *Methodology Walkthrough*
A walkthrough of how I turn a chaotic support-ticket queue into insight — export, clean, pivot — and the subtle finding hiding in a field most people glance past. Built on synthetic data to keep the focus on method.

**🎴 [Yu-Gi-Oh! World Championship Database](https://github.com/dianarchin/yugioh-world-championship-database)** — *Database &amp; Analysis*
A normalized PostgreSQL database tracking 21 years of competition. Started from a question — which regions and archetypes dominated, and how did the meta shift? — and answered it with SQL, Docker, and Tableau. Swap "decks" for products and it's a competitive-performance analysis.

**📚 [Goodreads Book Dashboard](https://github.com/dianarchin/book-dashboard)** — *EDA &amp; Visualization*
485 books treated as a dataset worth questioning: what drives a high rating, how habits shift, where patterns break. The real exercise was turning a vague "what's going on here?" into specific, answerable questions a non-technical reader gets at a glance.

**📈 [Yu-Gi-Oh! TCG Market Analysis](https://dianarchin.hashnode.dev/building-a-yu-gi-oh-tcg-market-analysis-dashboard-in-tableau)** — *Market Analysis*
A Tableau dashboard treating a collectibles market like any other product market — what's appreciating, what's losing value, where popularity and price diverge. The same demand and pricing questions an analyst asks of any business.
